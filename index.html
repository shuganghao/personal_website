Lin Gao (S'08-M'10-SM'16) is an Associate Professor at Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen, China. He received Ph.D. degree in Electronic Engineering (Advisors: Prof. Xinbing Wang and Prof. Youyun Xu) from Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU) in 2010, and worked as a Postdoc Research Fellow (Advisor: Prof. Jianwei Huang) in the Network Communications and Economics Lab (NCEL) at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) during 2010-2015. He received the IEEE ComSoc Asia-Pacific Outstanding Young Researcher Award in 2016. His main research interests are in the interdisciplinary area between game theory and AI, with applications in wireless communications, mobile computing, and networking.

Openings: We have openings for both Postdoc researchers and PhD students in the interdisciplinary research of mobile computing, machine learning, and game theory. Research topics include but are not limited to edge computing, crowd intelligence, network game theory, reinforcement learning, and federated learning.
